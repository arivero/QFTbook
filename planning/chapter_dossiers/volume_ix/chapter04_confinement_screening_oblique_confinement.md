# Volume IX, Chapter 4 Dossier: Confinement, Screening, and Oblique Confinement

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: define confinement through line-operator behavior and
  charge lattices rather than through informal local-order-parameter language.
- Immediate predecessor: line, surface, and domain-wall operators.
- Immediate successor: discrete theta terms and anomaly inflow.

## Definitions And Results

- Electric/magnetic line-charge lattice and Dirac pairing.
- Screening quotient by dynamical charges.
- Area and perimeter law definitions for renormalized line operators.
- Strong-coupling lattice area-law mechanism by character expansion.
- Electric, magnetic, and oblique confinement through condensed charge
  sublattices.

## Claim Ledger

1. Line labels depend on the global form of the gauge group.
2. Screening is a quotient by charges carried by dynamical objects.
3. Area/perimeter laws are asymptotic statements about renormalized line
   correlators.
4. Strong-coupling lattice area behavior follows from character expansion and
   Haar projection.
5. Oblique confinement is described by a dyonic condensed sublattice.
