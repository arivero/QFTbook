# Volume VIII, Chapter 5 Dossier: Cohomological Field Theories

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: define metric-independent cohomological QFT through
  an odd \(Q\)-complex and controlled \(Q\)-Stokes arguments.
- Immediate predecessor: Chern--Simons theory.
- Immediate successor: topological sigma models and twists of supersymmetric
  theories.

## Definitions And Results

- Cohomological QFT datum.
- Metric independence from \(Q\)-exact stress-tensor variation.
- Independence under \(Q\)-exact deformations.
- Finite-dimensional localization lemma with compact nondegenerate fixed
  locus.
- BV form for gauge cohomological theories.

## Symbols

| Symbol | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| \(\mathcal F\) | regulated field space or BV complex |
| \(Q\) | odd cohomological derivation |
| \(S\) | action |
| \(V\) | deformation functional |
| \(F\) | fixed locus of the \(Q\)-exact deformation |
| \(G_{\mu\nu}\) | local functional whose \(Q\)-variation is stress-tensor variation |

## Claim Ledger

1. Metric independence follows only when the metric variation is \(Q\)-exact
   and the measure/cycle has no \(Q\)-boundary contribution.
2. \(Q\)-exact deformation independence is Stokes' theorem in field space.
3. Localization requires compactness, nondegeneracy, and orientation data.
4. Gauge theories require BV rather than only a naive \(Q\)-complex.
