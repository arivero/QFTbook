# Volume VIII, Chapter 3 Dossier: BF Theory

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: first explicit cohomological gauge-theory example
  after the functorial target structure.
- Immediate predecessor: bordism functoriality and extraction from local QFT.
- Immediate successor: Chern--Simons theory and broader cohomological gauge
  theories.

## Definitions And Results

- Classical BF fields \(A\) and \(B\) on a principal \(G\)-bundle.
- BF action \(2\pi i\int \langle B\wedge F_A\rangle\).
- Equations of motion \(F_A=0\) and \(d_A B=0\).
- Ordinary gauge symmetry and \(B\)-field shift symmetry.
- Reducibility chain requiring BV ghosts-for-ghosts.
- Metric-independence conditions.
- Wilson and surface observables.
- Finite abelian BF theory as a rigorous finite-sum model.

## Claim Ledger

1. The BF equations of motion follow directly from varying \(A\) and \(B\).
2. The \(B\)-shift symmetry is reducible on the flat-connection locus.
3. Metric independence requires both metric-free classical action and
   anomaly-free gauge fixing/regularization.
4. Finite abelian BF theory provides a fully finite rigorous topological
   model.
