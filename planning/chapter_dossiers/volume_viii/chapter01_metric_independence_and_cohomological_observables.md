# Volume VIII, Chapter 1 Dossier: Metric Independence And Cohomological Observables

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: open the topological/cohomological volume from local
  stress-tensor variation and BRST-type cohomology.
- Immediate predecessor: gauge-theory BRST/BV material and CFT stress-tensor
  material.
- Immediate successor: Atiyah--Segal functoriality, BF theory, and
  Chern--Simons theory.

## Definitions And Results

- Metric variation of a QFT generating functional.
- \(Q\)-cohomological observables and \(Q\)-exact stress tensor condition.
- Proof that \(Q\)-closed correlators are invariant under smooth metric
  deformations modulo stated contact-term hypotheses.
- Distinction between cohomological field theory and fully extended functorial
  TQFT as a theorem target.

## Claim Ledger

1. Metric independence is derived from the stress-tensor variation formula.
2. The \(Q\)-exactness of \(T_{\mu\nu}\) is a sufficient cohomological
   mechanism for metric-independent correlators.
3. Contact terms at operator insertions require their own descent and
   renormalization analysis.
4. A functorial TQFT contains gluing data beyond metric-independent numbers.
