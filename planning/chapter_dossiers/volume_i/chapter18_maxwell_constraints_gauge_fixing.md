## Claims Established

- Maxwell theory is a theory of gauge-equivalence classes of vector-potential
  representatives, with \(F_{\mu\nu}\) as the local gauge-invariant field.
- The canonical momentum conjugate to \(A_0\) vanishes, giving a primary
  constraint; preserving it gives Gauss' law as a secondary constraint.
- These constraints are first-class and generate the infinitesimal
  representative change \(A_\mu\mapsto A_\mu+\partial_\mu\zeta\).
- Axial gauge turns the relevant constraints into a solvable second-class
  system for generic modes and leaves two canonical photon degrees of freedom.
- Covariant gauge fixing adds an invertible quadratic form and yields the
  standard photon propagator.
- Field-strength correlators are independent of the covariant gauge parameter.
