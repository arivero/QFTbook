# Volume X, Chapter 4 Dossier: Spectral Functions, Kubo Formulae, and Transport

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: derive transport coefficients from real-time thermal
  response before hydrodynamics is introduced.
- Immediate predecessor: Schwinger--Keldysh real-time formalism.
- Immediate successor: hydrodynamics from Ward identities and local
  equilibrium.

## Definitions And Results

- Retarded thermal correlator.
- Spectral density and finite-volume spectral representation.
- KMS fluctuation--dissipation relation.
- Linear response from Schwinger--Keldysh contour difference.
- Conductivity and shear-viscosity Kubo formulae.
- Open theorem boundary from Kubo formulae to hydrodynamics.

## Claim Ledger

1. Retarded response is the Schwinger--Keldysh branch difference and equals a
   commutator with a causal step function.
2. KMS implies the fluctuation--dissipation relation.
3. Conductivity and viscosity are low-frequency limits after contact terms
   and conserved-density mixings are specified.
4. Hydrodynamic control requires additional analyticity and limit-exchange
   hypotheses.
