## Claim Ledger

1. The KMS condition is an equilibrium condition on a state and a time
   evolution.
2. Finite-volume Gibbs traces satisfy KMS by trace cyclicity.
3. Detailed balance follows from KMS strip analyticity and Fourier
   transformation under stated temperedness hypotheses.
4. Linear response follows from first-order expansion of the perturbed
   time-evolution operator.
5. Transport coefficients are QFT correlator limits only when the relevant
   zero-frequency limits exist.
6. Hydrodynamic fields arise from conserved densities together with a
   long-wavelength state-family assumption and constitutive relations.
