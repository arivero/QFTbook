# Volume X, Chapter 3 Dossier: Real-Time Schwinger--Keldysh Formalism

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: introduce real-time generating functionals after
  Euclidean finite-temperature path integrals.
- Immediate predecessor: finite-temperature path integrals and Matsubara data.
- Immediate successor: spectral functions, Kubo formulae, and transport.

## Definitions And Results

- Closed-time-path generating functional \(Z[J_+,J_-]\).
- Forward and backward source evolution operators.
- Unitarity constraint \(Z[J,J]=1\).
- \(r/a\) source variables.
- Derivation of retarded response from source differentiation.
- KMS relation on the real-time contour.
- Status of Schwinger--Keldysh path integrals as regulated coordinate
  representations.

## Claim Ledger

1. The closed time path follows from a trace over forward and backward
   evolutions.
2. \(Z[J,J]=1\) follows from unitarity and normalization of \(\rho_0\).
3. Retarded response is obtained by differentiating with respect to the
   difference source and then setting the difference source to zero.
4. KMS relations require the thermal density matrix and analytic-domain
   hypotheses, not only a contour drawing.
