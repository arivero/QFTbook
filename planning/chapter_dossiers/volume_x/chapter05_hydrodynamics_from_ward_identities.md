# Volume X, Chapter 5 Dossier: Hydrodynamics from Ward Identities

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: derive the hydrodynamic equations and linearized
  modes from conservation laws and local thermodynamics after Kubo formulae
  have been defined.
- Immediate predecessor: spectral functions, Kubo formulae, and transport.
- Immediate successor: Schwinger--Keldysh hydrodynamic effective actions.

## Definitions And Results

- Local temperature, chemical potentials, and velocity field.
- Ideal stress tensor and current.
- Thermodynamic closure relations.
- Entropy-current conservation at ideal order.
- First-order viscous and diffusive constitutive relations.
- Shear diffusion and sound poles.

## Claim Ledger

1. Ideal hydrodynamics follows from Ward identities plus thermodynamic
   closure.
2. The ideal entropy current is conserved using the first law.
3. First-order transport coefficients are constrained by entropy production.
4. Hydrodynamic poles must match the low-frequency retarded correlators of
   the Kubo chapter under additional microscopic hypotheses.
