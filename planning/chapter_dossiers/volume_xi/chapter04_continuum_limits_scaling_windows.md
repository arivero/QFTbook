# Volume XI, Chapter 4 Dossier: Continuum Limits and Scaling Windows

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: define continuum limits of regulated lattice or
  cutoff theories as distributional limits satisfying reconstruction
  hypotheses.
- Immediate predecessor: lattice reflection positivity.
- Immediate successor: Wilson lattice gauge theory and numerical regulator
  frameworks.

## Definitions And Results

- Scaling trajectory in regulated action space.
- Renormalized smeared lattice observables.
- Constructive continuum limit.
- Scaling window and lattice correlation length.
- Linearized Wilsonian coordinates in a Banach-space setup.
- Status distinction among \(\phi^4_3\), the 3D Ising CFT, and
  \(\phi^4_4\).

## Claim Ledger

1. Continuum limits are distributional limits of renormalized lattice
   correlators.
2. OS reconstruction requires more than pointwise convergence of correlators.
3. Scaling windows require separation between lattice spacing and correlation
   length.
4. Fixed-point claims require an operational RG map and estimates in a stated
   function space.
