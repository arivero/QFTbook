# Volume XI, Chapter 1 Dossier: Constructive Status And Routes

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: launch the constructive/lattice/numerical volume and
  give the global status map requested by the open issues.
- Immediate predecessor: foundational OS/Wightman/AQFT chapters and lattice
  gauge-theory material.
- Immediate successor: detailed constructive examples, lattice reflection
  positivity, stochastic quantization, and numerical QFT.

## Definitions And Results

- Constructive realization in a specified framework.
- Three construction routes: cluster/phase-cell expansions, lattice scaling
  limits, and stochastic quantization or singular SPDE.
- Master status table for free fields, \(P(\phi)_2\), \(\phi^4_3\),
  sine--Gordon, Yukawa\(_2\), Gross--Neveu\(_2\), two-dimensional
  Yang--Mills, stochastic \(\phi^4_2/\phi^4_3\), the three-dimensional Ising
  CFT problem, scalar triviality, four-dimensional Yang--Mills, QCD, and the
  Standard Model.
- Reference-anchor paragraph for later deep construction chapters.
- Stochastic quantization opening with renormalized Langevin equation.

## Claim Ledger

1. A finite regulator is a construction only after a limit and framework
   verification are specified.
2. The status table separates completed constructions, finite regulators,
   triviality theorems, and open continuum-limit problems.
3. The three-dimensional Ising CFT is distinct from constructed massive
   \(\phi^4_3\).
4. The possibility of a four-dimensional UV-complete QFT matching formal
   \(\phi^4_4\) perturbation theory remains a separate open question.
5. Singular-SPDE methods give a dynamic route to Euclidean measures in
   constructed scalar cases.
