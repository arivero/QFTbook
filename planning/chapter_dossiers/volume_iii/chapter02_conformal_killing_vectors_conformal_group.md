# Volume III, Chapter 2 Dossier: Conformal Killing Vectors And The Conformal Group

## Logical Role

- Role in the monograph: derive the finite-dimensional conformal algebra in
  \(D\ge3\) from the conformal Killing equation and identify the global
  Euclidean group acting on flat-space separated-point correlators.
- Immediate predecessor: fixed points, source functionals, and conformal data.
- Immediate successor: stress tensor, Weyl structure, and improvement.

## Definitions And Results

The chapter establishes:

- the conformal Killing equation on \(\mathbb R^D\) with Euclidean metric
  \(\delta_{\mu\nu}\);
- the derivation that its local polynomial solutions in \(D\ge3\) are
  translations, rotations, dilatations, and special conformal fields;
- the commutation relations with a declared Euclidean-signature convention;
- the embedding of the connected conformal group into the appropriate
  orthogonal group acting on the projective null cone;
- the distinction between local conformal transformations and globally
  defined transformations on compactified Euclidean space;
- the hypotheses under which these transformations act on operator
  insertions and on separated-point distributions.

## Claims To Verify

1. The conformal Killing equation is an equation for vector fields preserving
   the metric up to a scalar Weyl factor.
2. In \(D\ge3\), differentiating the equation closes the solution space to
   polynomial vector fields of degree at most two.
3. The global group statement requires compactification data and excludes
   singular maps whose domain omits insertion points.
4. Euclidean conformal algebra conventions must be reconciled later with
   Hermitian Lorentzian and radial conventions.
