# Chapter 11: Finite-Volume Form Factors And Spectral Expansions

## Source Position

This chapter follows the bridge from integrable to nonintegrable
two-dimensional QFT by giving the finite-volume Hilbert-space normalization
needed to compare bootstrap form factors with correlation functions.

## Notation Inventory

- `L`: spatial circumference used as an infrared regulator.
- `theta_j`, `a_j`: rapidities and particle species.
- `Q_j(theta)`: Bethe-Yang phase equations.
- `rho_n(theta)`: Gaudin determinant density.
- `F^O`: infinite-volume form factor of a local operator `O`.
- `F^O_{2n,c}`: connected diagonal form factor.
- `n_a(theta)`: TBA occupation factor.

## Claim Ledger

- Defines finite-volume Bethe-Yang states and their Gaudin normalization.
- Gives the off-diagonal finite-volume form-factor relation with the stated
  exponential-correction boundary.
- Defines connected diagonal form factors through finite parts after removing
  kinematic-pole terms.
- Derives the finite-volume spectral expansion of two-point functions.
- States the thermal trace series as a TBA-compatible connected-form-factor
  expression with convergence obligations.
