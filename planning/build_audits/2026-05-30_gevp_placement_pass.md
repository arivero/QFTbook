# GEVP Placement Pass

Date: 2026-05-30

## Prompt

Xi flagged that the generalized eigenvalue problem discussion in the
\(\mathcal N=1\) SYM spectral chapter looked misplaced: GEVP is a general
finite-regulator spectral-extraction method, not a structure special to that
supersymmetric gauge theory.

## Changes Made

- Retitled the Volume XI lattice section from "Glueball Correlator Matrices
  and the GEVP" to "Finite-Volume Correlator Matrices and the GEVP".
- Added an opening paragraph stating that the GEVP applies to any
  reflection-positive finite-regulator channel with a transfer matrix and a
  finite source family; pure Yang--Mills glueballs are only the running
  example.
- In Volume VII Chapter 7b, reduced the pure-SYM GEVP discussion to a
  channel-specific application:
  \(C_+(t)\), \(C_-(t)\), and \(C_F(t)\) define the sources whose extracted
  pole locations are compared for supersymmetry restoration.
- Removed the duplicated finite-state GEVP diagonalization proof from the
  pure-SYM proposition and redirected that general linear-algebra analysis to
  the Volume XI finite-volume GEVP section.
- Moved the QCD spectroscopy basis-covariance proof out of the QCD chapter
  and into the general Volume XI GEVP section; the QCD chapter now explains
  only the QCD interpretation of source charts and overlap labels.
- Reframed the early lattice-Yang--Mills chapter so that pure-glue source
  spaces and continuum data are the Yang--Mills-specific content, while the
  GEVP method is referenced to Volume XI.
- Reframed the integrability-to-nonintegrability TCSA paragraph as the
  two-dimensional CFT-deformation instance of the general Hamiltonian
  truncation framework in Volume XI.
- Tightened the strict writing harness with a general-method placement rule.

## Resulting Logical Placement

- General method: Volume XI, finite-volume correlator matrices and GEVP.
- Pure \(\mathcal N=1\) SYM use: Volume VII, channel source construction and
  supersymmetry-restoration diagnostic.
