# Issue #444 \(M_\delta\) Versus Partial-Wave Notation

## Scope

- GitHub issue #444 flagged that \(M_\delta\), the
  \(\delta^{(d)}\)-normalized sharp-momentum connected kernel, could be
  confused with partial-wave amplitude notation in nearby chapters.

## Resolution

- Volume I Chapter 14 now gives an explicit notation table at the first
  definition of \(M_\delta\):
  - \(M_\delta(\beta|\alpha)\) is the sharp-momentum kernel in a
    \(\delta^{(d)}\)-normalized basis;
  - \(\mathcal M(f|i)\) is the invariant amplitude after external
    normalization factors;
  - \(a_\ell(s)\) is the ordered \(16\pi\) partial-wave coefficient of
    \(\mathcal M\);
  - \(S_\ell(s)\) is the partial-wave scattering eigenvalue.
- Volume II Chapter 3 now states at the first partial-wave expansion that the
  coefficient is deliberately called \(a_\ell\), with \(M_\delta\) reserved for
  the delta-normalized sharp-momentum kernel.
- The Volume I and Volume II chapter dossiers record the convention so future
  edits do not reintroduce \(M_\ell\)-style ambiguity.

## Verification Target

The reader should be able to distinguish the normalization-dependent
sharp-momentum kernel from invariant-amplitude partial-wave coordinates before
using the bound-state and resonance partial-wave formulas.
