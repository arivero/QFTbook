# 2026-05-30 Large-Charge Moduli-Space Bridge

## Changes

- Clarified Open Problem 93.3 as a problem of constructing the full
  supersymmetric vacuum datum from regulated continuum QFT and intrinsic
  operator/state data.
- Refined the same open problem as a supersymmetric test case of the broader
  passage from Wightman-type local QFT data to Kontsevich-Segal-type
  geometric amplitude data, including boundary state spaces, sewing maps,
  reflection pairings, and background-field dependence.
- Added a section on fixed charge as a probe of a branch, separating:
  chiral-ring characters, which probe reduced holomorphic coordinate data,
  from large-charge sectors, which probe asymptotic low-energy EFT data on
  charged regions of a branch.
- Defined the charge operators, fixed-charge Hilbert sectors, chemical
  potentials, branch isometry vector fields, the charged homogeneous saddle,
  and the Noether map from branch EFT data to fixed-charge energy.
- Connected the conformal version to the state-operator theorem already
  developed in Volume III: large-charge operators of definite scaling
  dimension encode fixed-charge cylinder energy eigenstates when the relevant
  state lies in the local-operator subspace.
- Recorded chiral-ring characters and fixed-charge spectra as probes of this
  Wightman-to-KS reconstruction, rather than as the reconstruction itself.

## Remaining Boundary

The added section is a mechanism-level formulation, not a closed theorem.  A
future chapter on large-charge EFT should prove precise error estimates in
specific controlled classes of CFTs, including the hypotheses on the charged
saddle, transverse fluctuation spectrum, Wess-Zumino terms, and uniform
suppression of omitted higher-derivative operators.
