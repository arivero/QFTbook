## Substance

The Schwinger-model current-sector bosonization input has been converted from
`quotedtheorem` to a local theorem/proof for the gauge-invariant derivative
current algebra.  The proof has three explicit parts:

1. The current normalization is fixed from the equal-time Schwinger term
   obtained by gauge-invariant point splitting.
2. The Euclidean Dirac current source functional is derived by Hodge
   decomposing a Schwartz background one-form \(b=\dd\lambda+*\dd\rho\).  The
   vector Ward identity removes \(\dd\lambda\); the transverse source is an
   axial pure gauge and is removed by an axial rotation.  The Fujikawa
   Jacobian with the chapter's anomaly convention gives the exact quadratic
   transverse functional.
3. The scalar Gaussian integral with
   \(J^\mu=\pi^{-1/2}\epsilon^{\mu\nu}\partial_\nu\varphi\) gives the same
   source functional, hence the same current distributions after analytic
   continuation.

The compact period \(\varphi\sim\varphi+\sqrt\pi\) is now described as data of
the vertex-operator extension needed for charged fermion operators.  It is
not presented as something determined by the derivative current algebra alone.

## Remaining Boundary

The proof covers the local gauge-invariant current subalgebra used in the
Schwinger-model gauge dynamics.  A full construction of all charged vertex
operator sectors, spin structures, and finite-volume charge lattices remains a
separate two-dimensional CFT construction.
