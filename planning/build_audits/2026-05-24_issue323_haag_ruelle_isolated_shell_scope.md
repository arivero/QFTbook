# Issue #323 Audit: Haag--Ruelle Isolated Mass Shell Scope

## Scope

- GitHub issue: `#323`, asking for the physical scope of the isolated massive
  one-particle shell hypothesis in Haag--Ruelle scattering.
- Manuscript files:
  - `monograph/tex/volumes/volume_i/chapter12_haag_ruelle_scattering_theory.tex`
  - `monograph/tex/volumes/volume_iv/chapter05_haag_ruelle_and_mathematical_scattering.tex`

## Resolution

- Added a scope remark immediately after the isolated-shell definition in the
  introductory Haag--Ruelle chapter.
- Added a theorem-adjacent scope remark immediately after the algebraic
  Haag--Ruelle theorem in the mathematical scattering chapter.
- The remarks state that the isolated shell is a nonperturbative spectral
  theorem or hypothesis about the model, not a generic consequence of a formal
  Lagrangian.
- Examples and nonexamples are classified:
  - free massive fields satisfy the condition;
  - constructive low-dimensional massive scalar models, such as \(P(\phi)_2\)
    and massive \(\lambda\phi^4_3\), supply examples in regimes where the
    Wightman construction, mass gap, and one-particle spectral gap are proved;
  - four-dimensional perturbative \(\lambda\phi^4\) gives formal pole evidence
    but not a nonperturbative Hilbert-space theorem;
  - QED charged sectors are infraparticle sectors rather than sharp Wigner
    mass shells;
  - QCD colored quark/gluon shells are absent from the gauge-invariant
    observable spectrum, while stable hadrons may be isolated;
  - resonances and unstable particles are not Haag--Ruelle external particles.
- Updated the Haag--Ruelle chapter dossier to keep this distinction in future
  development passes.
