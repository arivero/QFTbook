# 2026-05-25 Energy Correlator Calorimetric Coordinates Pass

GitHub issues: #519 and #526, concerning energy correlators, infrared-safe

## Manuscript Change

Volume II, Chapter 19b now contains a new section,
`Energy Correlators as Calorimetric Coordinates`.  The pass formulates a
final state as a positive finite energy measure on the celestial sphere,
defines smeared \(k\)-point energy-correlator functionals, and separates
detector observables from partonic histories.

The new technical results are:

- Definition `def:smeared-energy-correlator-functional`, giving the
  \(K\)-smeared \(k\)-point functional
  \(\mathcal C_K^{(k)}(\mu)\) for \(K\in C((S^2)^k)\).
- Lemma `lem:smeared-energy-correlator-continuity`, proving weak
  continuity and explicit soft and collinear continuity estimates for
  Lipschitz smeared correlators on finite-energy measure balls.
- Theorem `thm:energy-correlator-polynomial-density`, proving by the
  Riesz representation theorem and Stone-Weierstrass that the algebra
  generated by smeared energy moments is uniformly dense in continuous
  finite-resolution calorimetric observables on \(\mathcal M_E(S^2)\).

The classification section of the chapter now points back to this theorem
when explaining what nonperturbative input energy correlators require:
stress-tensor energy-flow operators on the physical Hilbert space plus
angular test kernels defining continuous functionals of the calorimetric
measure.

## Boundary

The theorem is deliberately finite-resolution.  It applies to continuous
functions of a positive finite-energy measure.  Sharp cuts, exact jet-counting
boundaries, and distributional angular kernels such as the unsmeared EEC
require limiting arguments and boundary analysis.  Graph-indexed energy-flow
polynomial bases require a further approximation statement for the chosen
family of angular kernels and detector resolution.
