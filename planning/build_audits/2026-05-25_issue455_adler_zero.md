## Manuscript Changes

- Added `Soft-Pion Ward Identity and the Adler Zero` before the low-energy
  pion-scattering section.
- Stated the non-singlet axial-current Ward identity as a distributional
  identity with contact terms generated by
  \(\delta_A^a\Pi^{a_j}\).
- Isolated the current-channel pion pole using
  \(\langle 0|A_a^\mu(0)|\pi^b(p)\rangle=i f_\pi p^\mu\delta^{ab}\).
- Defined the hard external-pion LSZ operation \(\mathcal L_{\{p_j\}}\) and
  derived the current-algebra soft-pion identity
  \[
    f_\pi \mathcal M_{n+1}^{a,a_1,\ldots,a_n}
    =
    \mathcal C_a+O(q).
  \]
- Proved the Adler zero for connected purely pionic amplitudes in the exact
  chiral limit, under explicit existence and nonexceptional-soft-limit
  assumptions, by using the absence of a one-pion-to-one-pion term in the
  broken axial action on the chiral coset.
- Added a schematic figure showing the axial-current insertion, pion pole,
  Ward-contact terms, and hard-LSZ conclusion.
- Connected the theorem to the four-pion result
  \(A(s,t,u)=s/f_\pi^2+O(E^4/f_\pi^4)\) and to the displaced massive Adler
  point at leading order.
