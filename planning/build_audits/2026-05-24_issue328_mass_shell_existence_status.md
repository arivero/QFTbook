## Mathematical Point

Issue #328 correctly identified that the notation
\(\Hilb_1=E(\Sigma_m^+)\Hilb\) must not smuggle in existence of a stable
particle.  The manuscript now states separately:

- \(E(\Sigma_m^+)\Hilb\ne0\), the nonzero one-particle spectral projection;
- existence of an open neighborhood \(N\) with
  \(\operatorname{Spec}(P)\cap N=\Sigma_m^+\), the isolation condition;
- a Kallen-Lehmann atom \(Z_\phi\delta_{m^2}\) is a field-dependent detection
  of that shell, while a continuous threshold with no atom supplies no
  normalizable Wigner one-particle vector for that field.

## Scope Boundary

The Haag--Ruelle chapter now says explicitly that if the projection is zero,
the corresponding scattering construction is vacuous, and if the shell is not
isolated ordinary massive Haag--Ruelle estimates do not apply.  The examples
are stated as model-specific spectral theorems or hypotheses, not as formal
consequences of Lagrangian notation or perturbative poles.
