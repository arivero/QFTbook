# 2026-05-27 Issue #628 Dense Non-Fermi-Liquid Self-Energy Pass

## Scope

This pass deepens Volume X, Chapter 12 by adding the cold dense unpaired
quark self-energy generated by Landau-damped magnetic gluon exchange.  The
goal is to make the non-Fermi-liquid statement a controlled HDET derivation
with explicit conventions, not a slogan about dense matter.

## Substantive Additions

- Added a controlled HDL non-Fermi-liquid regime at \(T=0\), with
  \(|p_0|,|\ell_\parallel|\ll\Lambda_{\rm H}\ll\mu_q\), \(g(\mu_q)\ll1\),
  the trace-delta fundamental Casimir \(C_F=(N_c^2-1)/N_c\), and the
  Landau-damped transverse propagator.
- Defined the leading non-Fermi-liquid self-energy as the nonanalytic
  \(ip_0\log |p_0|\) term in the projected patch inverse propagator, with
  analytic local HDET counterterms explicitly excluded.
- Derived
  \[
    \Sigma_{\rm NFL}(ip_0,0)
    =
    ip_0 \frac{g^2C_F}{12\pi^2}
    \log\frac{\Lambda_{\rm NFL}}{|p_0|}
    +O(ip_0)
  \]
  and the \(SU(3)\) trace-delta specialization
  \(\lambda_{\rm NFL}=2g^2/(9\pi^2)\).
- Made the \(1/3\) Landau-damped collinear logarithm explicit and separated
  the coefficient from finite matching data in \(\Lambda_{\rm NFL}\).
- Added the logarithmic patch-residue consequence
  \(Z(p_0)\sim[\lambda_{\rm NFL}\log(\Lambda_{\rm NFL}/|p_0|)]^{-1}\).
- Clarified that this is an unpaired high-density perturbative statement and
  does not by itself construct gauge-invariant observables in all dense
  phases.
- Added exact arithmetic checks for the trace-delta \(SU(3)\) coefficient,
  the factorization into the patch prefactor and collinear \(1/3\), and the
  inverse-log residue bookkeeping.
