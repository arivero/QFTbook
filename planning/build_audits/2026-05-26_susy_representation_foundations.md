## Scope

This pass strengthens Volume VII Chapter 01, `Supersymmetry Algebras and
Representations`, by replacing implicit representation-theory shorthand with
explicit Hilbert-space hypotheses, oscillator constructions, and positivity
arguments.  The additions are intrinsic QFT representation theory; no
string-theoretic compactification input is used.

## Substantive Edits

- Added the massive `4d` `N=1` rest-frame oscillator module as a proposition:
  the one-particle supersymmetry module is
  `V_j \otimes Lambda^bullet C^2`, has total dimension `4(2j+1)`, and has
  equal bosonic and fermionic dimensions `2(2j+1)`.
- Derived the spin decomposition
  `V_j \otimes C^2 = V_{j+1/2} \oplus V_{j-1/2}`, with the negative-spin
  summand omitted at `j=0`, and paired it with an exact
  Clebsch-Gordan dimension check.
- Added the massless rank-one norm-matrix construction for
  `p=(E,0,0,E)`, proving that the zero-norm supercharge is quotiented out and
  the irreducible massless multiplet is generated by a single oscillator.
- Added the central-charge/BPS positivity proposition: after singular-value
  reduction of the central-charge matrix, each block has eigenvalues
  `2(m +/- z_s)`, giving the BPS inequalities `m >= z_s` and explaining
  shortening at saturation.
- Added `calculation-checks/susy_representation_checks.py` and updated the
  Chapter 01 dossier and calculation-check README.
