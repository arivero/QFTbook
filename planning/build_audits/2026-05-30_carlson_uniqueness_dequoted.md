## Substance

The half-plane Carlson uniqueness input in the Regge discussion has been
converted from `quotedtheorem` to a local theorem with proof.  The statement
keeps the strict exponential-type hypotheses in the closed right half-plane
and on the imaginary axis.  The proof uses the right-half-plane
Carleman--Jensen zero-density estimate, compares it with the lower density
forced by zeros at every positive integer, and records the sharpness of the
\(\pi\) threshold by the model function \(\sin \pi J\).

The Regge application now refers to the proved theorem rather than to a quoted
input.  It still explicitly separates uniqueness from existence: Carlson
uniqueness only controls a complex-\(J\) continuation after the continuation,
common pole subtraction, and growth class have been supplied.

## Remaining Boundary

The existence of the Regge interpolation and its pole/vertical-growth
properties is not inferred from the physical partial-wave series.  It remains
declared as separate analytic input, as required for a non-slogan treatment of
Regge theory.
