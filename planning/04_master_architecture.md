# Master Architecture

## Governing Constraint

The architecture follows the logical order of Xi Yin's QFT notes. The
monograph may deepen, expand, formalize, and connect topics beyond the notes,
but it must preserve the order of construction.

The spine is conceptual, not merely topical:

1. local quantum data and spacetime symmetry;
2. fields and local observables;
3. path-integral constructions for Green functions;
4. spectral representation and particle content;
5. perturbative Green functions;
6. nonperturbative asymptotic states and S-matrix;
7. LSZ;
8. perturbative scattering amplitudes;
9. spin, fermions, massless particles, gauge redundancy, and QED;
10. renormalization, RG, nonabelian gauge theory, anomalies, EFT, and IR;
11. conformal field theory and its modern structures;
12. comparison with mathematical frameworks and frontier extensions.

## Global Rules

- Kallen--Lehmann spectral representation appears early.
- Feynman diagrams for Green functions may appear before scattering.
- The S-matrix is first defined nonperturbatively.
- LSZ follows the nonperturbative S-matrix.
- Feynman diagrams for S-matrix elements appear only after LSZ.
- Axiomatic frameworks are discussed as framework comparisons and theorem
  sources, not as the book's single opening foundation.
- Every volume may include mathematical interludes, but an interlude must serve
  the local construction rather than replace the spine.
