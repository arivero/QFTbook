# Mathematical Frameworks And Their Domains

## Status

Existing axiomatic and structural frameworks for QFT are indispensable, but
none is the single strict foundation of the monograph and none is treated as a
privileged court of appeal.  A framework earns its place only by producing
nontrivial physics or mathematics: precise data, theorems, constructions,
examples, comparison maps, or sharply formulated open problems.  The manuscript
uses frameworks to formulate claims, clarify domains, study relationships,
and build substantial examples, while the logical order of the book follows
the construction of the subject.
No axiomatic framework is treated as sacred.  A framework earns text only when
it computes, constrains, classifies, constructs, compares, or exposes an
obstruction in actual QFT.

## Working Principle

For any theorem or construction, state:

- the framework in which it lives;
- the data of the framework;
- the hypotheses needed;
- the conclusion obtained;
- the relation, if known, to other frameworks;
- the examples in which the framework supplies genuine information;
- the physics or mathematics learned from using it;
- what remains open or outside the framework.

## Wightman Framework

Role:

- local operator-valued distributions on Minkowski space;
- Hilbert space, Poincare covariance, spectrum condition, vacuum, locality,
  and positivity;
- reconstruction from Wightman functions under precise assumptions.

Use:

- early local-field chapters;
- spectral representation;
- spin-statistics, CPT, Reeh--Schlieder, and scattering comparisons where
  hypotheses are stated.

Caution:

- point fields require domain and distributional care;
- known rigorous constructions are limited in four dimensions;
- gauge theories require careful treatment of physical Hilbert spaces,
  indefinite metrics, or gauge-invariant observables.

## Osterwalder--Schrader And Euclidean Frameworks

Role:

- Euclidean Schwinger functions;
- reflection positivity;
- reconstruction of Lorentzian Hilbert-space QFT under OS hypotheses.

Use:

- Wick rotation chapters;
- Euclidean path-integral chapters;
- constructive and lattice comparisons.

Caution:

- Euclidean correlation functions are not automatically Lorentzian QFT data;
- reflection positivity is structural, not optional decoration;
- analytic continuation must be justified.

## Algebraic QFT

Role:

- nets of local algebras assigned to spacetime regions;
- locality, covariance, isotony, states, representations, and sectors;
- powerful framework for locality, superselection, and structural theorems.

Use:

- local observable language;
- comparison with field-coordinate descriptions;
- later discussion of sectors, duality, modular theory, and curved spacetime.

Caution:

- local algebras may encode observables more naturally than point fields;
- relation to Lagrangian/path-integral constructions is subtle;
- not every physically used construction has a settled AQFT formulation.

## Perturbative AQFT

Role:

- perturbative construction of interacting local algebras;
- causal factorization;
- microlocal control of products and renormalization;
- precise formal power series framework.

Use:

- later comparison for perturbative locality and renormalization;
- BRST/BV treatment of gauge theories.

Caution:

- formal power series are not nonperturbative constructions;
- infrared and global issues require separate hypotheses.

## Constructive And Lattice Frameworks

Role:

- regulated definitions;
- continuum-limit questions;
- reflection positivity and probability measures in Euclidean settings;
- existence results in low dimensions and selected models.

Use:

- Wilsonian RG;
- statistical field theory;
- continuum limits and phases.

Caution:

- regulator-dependent objects are not automatically continuum theories;
- convergence must be stated in a specified topology or observable class.

## Factorization And Functorial Frameworks

Role:

- local-to-global algebraic organization;
- perturbative field theories through factorization algebras;
- topological and extended structures through functorial methods.

Use:

- advanced volumes;
- defects, OPE-like structures, chiral algebras, and topological sectors.

Caution:

- relation to Lorentzian Hilbert-space QFT and scattering is not a solved
  universal equivalence;
- categorical elegance does not replace analytic hypotheses.

## Kontsevich--Segal-Type Frameworks

Role:

- analytic and categorical viewpoint on Wick rotation and complexified
  spacetimes;
- possible bridge among Euclidean, Lorentzian, and topological structures.

Use:

- advanced comparison chapters;
- discussion of open foundations.

Caution:

- this is frontier material;
- definitions and equivalences must be stated with their precise current
  status.

## Comparison Questions To Track

- When do Wightman and OS data determine the same physical theory?
- Which AQFT nets arise from known Lagrangian or path-integral constructions?
- How do gauge theories fit Hilbert-space, algebraic, and Euclidean
  frameworks simultaneously?
- Which perturbative constructions admit nonperturbative completions?
- What is the correct framework for defects, boundaries, generalized
  symmetries, and higher-categorical structures in interacting QFT?
- What axioms capture both local QFT and the analytic structures needed for
  scattering, Wick rotation, and quantum gravity interfaces?

These questions should be kept visible without allowing any one incomplete
framework to dominate the logical foundation of the monograph.
