\chapter{Schwinger--Keldysh Hydrodynamic Effective Actions}
\label{ch:thermal-sk-hydrodynamic-effective-actions}

\ConventionsRealTime

Hydrodynamic effective actions are real-time generating functionals for
conserved densities in the Schwinger--Keldysh framework.  Their variables are
doubled, their action is complex, and their coefficients are fixed by Ward
identities, positivity, and the KMS condition.

\section{Doubled Sources and Average-Difference Variables}

For a conserved \(U(1)\) current, introduce two background gauge fields
\[
  A_{1\mu},\qquad A_{2\mu}
\]
on the forward and backward time contours.  Define
\[
  A_{r\mu}=\frac12(A_{1\mu}+A_{2\mu}),
  \qquad
  A_{a\mu}=A_{1\mu}-A_{2\mu}.
\]
The Schwinger--Keldysh generating functional is
\[
  Z[A_1,A_2]=\operatorname{Tr}\!\left(
  U[A_1]\rho_0U[A_2]^\dagger
  \right).
\]
Unitarity gives
\[
  Z[A,A]=1,
\]
and therefore the effective action \(I_{\mathrm{SK}}\) obeys
\[
  I_{\mathrm{SK}}[A_r,A_a=0]=0 .
\]

\section{Hydrodynamic Phase Field}

A hydrodynamic effective action for a conserved charge contains phase fields
\[
  \varphi_1,\qquad \varphi_2
\]
with gauge-invariant combinations
\[
  B_{s\mu}=A_{s\mu}+\partial_\mu\varphi_s,
  \qquad s=1,2 .
\]
In \(r/a\) variables,
\[
  B_{r\mu}=A_{r\mu}+\partial_\mu\varphi_r,
  \qquad
  B_{a\mu}=A_{a\mu}+\partial_\mu\varphi_a .
\]
Gauge invariance under
\[
  A_{s\mu}\mapsto A_{s\mu}+\partial_\mu\lambda_s,\qquad
  \varphi_s\mapsto\varphi_s-\lambda_s
\]
implies conservation of the current obtained by varying with respect to
\(A_{a\mu}\).

\section{Diffusion Action}

For a fluid at rest with susceptibility \(\chi>0\), conductivity
\(\sigma\geq0\), and diffusion constant
\[
  D=\frac{\sigma}{\chi},
\]
the quadratic effective action for charge diffusion may be written
\[
  I_{\mathrm{diff}}
  =
  \int dt\,d^{d-1}x\,
  \left[
  \chi B_{a0}B_{r0}
  -\sigma B_{ai}\partial_i B_{r0}
  +\ii T\sigma B_{ai}B_{ai}
  \right],
\]
up to terms with more derivatives or more powers of \(a\)-type fields.  The
imaginary part is nonnegative:
\[
  \operatorname{Im} I_{\mathrm{diff}}
  =
  T\sigma\int dt\,d^{d-1}x\, B_{ai}B_{ai}\geq0 .
\]
This inequality is the finite-action expression of noise positivity.

\section{Equation of Motion and Retarded Correlator}

Set external sources to zero after variation.  Varying with respect to
\(\varphi_a\) gives
\[
  \partial_t n-D\nabla^2 n=0,
  \qquad
  n=\chi\,\partial_t\varphi_r .
\]
To compute the retarded density correlator, keep \(A_{a0}\) as the source
for \(n\) and solve the linear equation in Fourier variables
\((\omega,\mathbf k)\).  The result is
\[
  G_R^{nn}(\omega,\mathbf k)
  =
  \frac{\chi D\mathbf k^2}{D\mathbf k^2-\ii\omega}.
\]
It has the hydrodynamic pole
\[
  \omega=-\ii D\mathbf k^2 .
\]
The symmetric correlator follows from the quadratic \(a\)-field term and
satisfies the fluctuation--dissipation relation of
Chapter~\ref{ch:thermal-spectral-kubo-transport}.

\section{Dynamical KMS Condition}

For a thermal state at inverse temperature \(\beta\), the effective action
must implement the KMS relation.  At the classical derivative level, this is
encoded by the transformation
\[
  \widetilde B_{r\mu}(t,\mathbf x)=B_{r\mu}(-t,\mathbf x),
  \qquad
  \widetilde B_{a\mu}(t,\mathbf x)
  =
  B_{a\mu}(-t,\mathbf x)
  +\ii\beta\,\partial_t B_{r\mu}(-t,\mathbf x),
\]
with time-reversal signs inserted according to tensor parity.  Requiring
\[
  I_{\mathrm{SK}}[\widetilde B_r,\widetilde B_a]
  =
  I_{\mathrm{SK}}[B_r,B_a]
\]
relates dissipative coefficients to noise coefficients.  In the diffusion
action above it fixes the coefficient of \(\ii B_{ai}B_{ai}\) to
\(T\sigma\).

\section{Beyond Diffusion}

For stress-tensor hydrodynamics the variables include maps from a fluid
worldvolume into spacetime, a doubled metric, and possible charge phases.  The
same principles apply: doubled diffeomorphism invariance gives Ward
identities, \(I[A,A]=0\) encodes unitarity, the imaginary part gives
positivity, and dynamical KMS relates transport and noise.  Classifying all
higher-derivative terms is a local cohomology problem in the doubled
hydrodynamic variables.

\begin{openproblem}[Microscopic derivation of SK hydrodynamic action]
\label{op:microscopic-sk-hydrodynamic-action}
Starting from a continuum QFT with a KMS state, derive the hydrodynamic
Schwinger--Keldysh effective action as a controlled long-wavelength limit of
connected real-time correlators, including the topology in which the
derivative expansion is asymptotic.
\end{openproblem}
