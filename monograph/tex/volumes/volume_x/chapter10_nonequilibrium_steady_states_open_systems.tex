\chapter{Nonequilibrium Steady States And Open-System Limits}
\label{ch:thermal-nonequilibrium-steady-states-open-systems}

\ConventionsRealTime

This chapter introduces nonequilibrium steady states and open-system limits as
QFT objects.  The definitions are made through states, correlation functions,
source protocols, and controlled scaling limits, rather than through
thermodynamic intuition alone.

\section{Steady-State Datum}

Let \(\mathcal A\) be the observable algebra of a real-time QFT and let
\(\tau_t\) be time evolution.  A steady state is a positive normalized
functional
\[
  \omega_{\mathrm{ss}}:\mathcal A\to\C
\]
such that
\[
  \omega_{\mathrm{ss}}(\tau_t(A))=\omega_{\mathrm{ss}}(A)
\]
for all observables \(A\) in its domain.  A nonequilibrium steady state has
nonzero currents or unequal reservoir parameters while remaining invariant
under time translations.  Local stationarity means that time-translated local
correlators are invariant as distributions.

\section{Reservoir Construction}

Consider a QFT on a line prepared at \(t=0\) by joining two half-systems with
KMS states at \((\beta_L,\mu_L)\) and \((\beta_R,\mu_R)\).  A candidate
steady state is the weak limit
\[
  \omega_{\mathrm{ss}}(A)
  =
  \lim_{t\to\infty}\omega_0(\tau_t(A))
\]
for local \(A\) near the junction.  Existence of the limit, independence of
the microscopic joining protocol, and clustering are separate assertions.
When conserved densities propagate ballistically, the steady state may be
described by occupation functions depending on ray \(x/t\).  At \(x/t=0\) the
state is the central nonequilibrium steady state.

\section{Entropy Production}

Let \(J_E\) be the energy current from left to right and \(J_Q\) a conserved
charge current.  For reservoirs with inverse temperatures \(\beta_{L,R}\) and
chemical potentials \(\mu_{L,R}\), the steady entropy production rate is
\[
  \dot S
  =
  (\beta_R-\beta_L)\omega_{\mathrm{ss}}(J_E)
  -
  (\beta_R\mu_R-\beta_L\mu_L)\omega_{\mathrm{ss}}(J_Q).
\]
This formula follows from the first law applied to the reservoirs: the right
reservoir gains energy \(J_E\) and charge \(J_Q\), while the left reservoir
loses the same amounts.  Positivity of \(\dot S\) is a dynamical property of
the constructed state and its reservoirs.

\section{Open-System Scaling Limit}

Let a system algebra \(\mathcal A_S\) couple weakly to an environment
\(\mathcal A_E\) by
\[
  H=H_S+H_E+\lambda\sum_a S_a\otimes E_a .
\]
Assume the environment is stationary and has rapidly decaying connected
correlators.  In the weak-coupling scaling
\[
  \lambda\to0,\qquad t=\lambda^{-2}\tau,
\]
the reduced system state may converge to a Markovian semigroup
\[
  \rho_S(\tau)=\ee^{\tau\mathcal L}\rho_S(0).
\]
Complete positivity and trace preservation give the GKSL form
\[
  \mathcal L(\rho)
  =
  -\ii[H_{\mathrm{eff}},\rho]
  +
  \sum_\alpha
  \left(
    L_\alpha\rho L_\alpha^\dagger
    -\frac12\{L_\alpha^\dagger L_\alpha,\rho\}
  \right).
\]
The operators \(L_\alpha\) are determined by Fourier transforms of
environment correlators at Bohr frequencies of \(H_S\).

\section{Schwinger--Keldysh Influence Functional}

In field theory the environment can be integrated out on a closed time path,
producing an influence functional
\[
  \ee^{\ii S_{\mathrm{IF}}[\phi_+,\phi_-]}.
\]
Unitarity imposes
\[
  S_{\mathrm{IF}}[\phi,\phi]=0,
\]
and positivity of the reduced density matrix imposes constraints on the
imaginary part of \(S_{\mathrm{IF}}\).  Expanding in
\[
  \phi_r=\frac12(\phi_++\phi_-),\qquad
  \phi_a=\phi_+-\phi_-,
\]
the quadratic open-system action has the form
\[
  S_{\mathrm{eff}}
  =
  \int\phi_a D_R\phi_r
  +
  \frac{\ii}{2}\int\phi_a N\phi_a+\cdots,
\]
where \(D_R\) is a retarded kernel and \(N\) is a noise kernel.  KMS
environments relate \(N\) to the anti-Hermitian part of \(D_R\).

\section{Hydrodynamic Open Systems}

If the system has conserved densities broken only by weak coupling to an
environment, the hydrodynamic equations acquire relaxation and noise terms:
\[
  \partial_t n+\nabla\cdot j=-\gamma(n-n_{\mathrm{eq}})+\xi.
\]
The noise satisfies
\[
  \langle\xi(t,\mathbf x)\xi(0,\mathbf 0)\rangle
  =
  2\gamma\chi T\,\delta(t)\delta(\mathbf x)
\]
for a KMS bath with susceptibility \(\chi\), in the Markovian limit.  This
relation follows from requiring the stationary solution of the Fokker-Planck
equation to have the equilibrium Gaussian weight for \(n\).

\begin{openproblem}[Continuum nonequilibrium states]
\label{op:continuum-nonequilibrium-steady-states}
Construct nonequilibrium steady states and open-system limits in interacting
continuum QFT with local algebras, KMS reservoirs, real-time correlators,
entropy production, hydrodynamic scaling limits, and complete-positivity
constraints derived from the microscopic dynamics.
\end{openproblem}
