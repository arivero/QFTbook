\chapter{Real-Time Schwinger--Keldysh Formalism}
\label{ch:thermal-schwinger-keldysh}

\ConventionsRealTime

Euclidean thermal correlators encode equilibrium data on the thermal circle.
Transport and response require real-time correlation functions.  The
Schwinger--Keldysh construction gives a generating functional for real-time
ordered products in an initial state without assuming a vacuum.

\section{Closed Time Path}

Let a quantum system have Hilbert space \(\Hilb\), Hamiltonian \(H\), and
initial density operator \(\rho_0\) with \(\operatorname{Tr}\rho_0=1\).  For
an operator \(O(t)\) coupled to a source \(J(t)\), define two time-evolution
operators
\[
  U_J(t_f,t_i)
  =
  \operatorname{T}\exp\!\left(
  -\ii\int_{t_i}^{t_f}dt\,\bigl(H-J(t)O\bigr)\right),
\]
\[
  U_J^\dagger(t_f,t_i)
  =
  \widetilde{\operatorname{T}}\exp\!\left(
  \ii\int_{t_i}^{t_f}dt\,\bigl(H-J(t)O\bigr)\right),
\]
where \(\operatorname{T}\) and \(\widetilde{\operatorname{T}}\) denote time
and anti-time ordering.  The closed-time-path generating functional is
\[
  Z[J_+,J_-]
  =
  \operatorname{Tr}\!\left(
  U_{J_+}(t_f,t_i)\rho_0 U_{J_-}^{\dagger}(t_f,t_i)
  \right).
\]
The \(+\) and \(-\) labels are contour labels, not independent physical
systems.

\section{Unitarity Constraint}

Setting \(J_+=J_-=J\) gives
\[
  Z[J,J]
  =
  \operatorname{Tr}\!\left(U_J\rho_0U_J^\dagger\right)
  =
  \operatorname{Tr}\rho_0=1.
\]
This identity is the real-time normalization constraint.  Functional
derivatives of \(Z\) with unequal sources generate contour-ordered
correlators; the equality above imposes relations among them.  Any effective
Schwinger--Keldysh action must preserve this identity, or must state the
controlled approximation that violates it.

\section{Retarded Functions}

Introduce
\[
  J_r=\frac12(J_++J_-),\qquad
  J_a=J_+-J_- .
\]
The expectation value of \(O\) in the presence of the physical source \(J_r\)
is obtained by differentiating with respect to \(J_a\) and then setting
\(J_a=0\).  Expanding to first order around \(J_r=0\) gives the retarded
response
\[
  \delta\langle O(t)\rangle
  =
  \int dt'\,G_R(t,t')\,\delta J_r(t'),
\]
where
\[
  G_R(t,t')
  =
  -\ii\,\theta(t-t')\operatorname{Tr}\rho_0[O(t),O(t')].
\]
The step function is a consequence of the operator ordering in \(U_J\):
varying the forward source at time \(t'\) changes later Heisenberg operators
but cancels against the backward variation for earlier times.

\section{KMS State on the Contour}

For the thermal state \(\rho_\beta=Z(\beta)^{-1}e^{-\beta H}\), cyclicity of
the trace gives the KMS relation
\[
  \operatorname{Tr}\rho_\beta A(t)B(0)
  =
  \operatorname{Tr}\rho_\beta B(0)A(t+\ii\beta)
\]
for operators whose analytic continuation is controlled in the stated strip.
In Schwinger--Keldysh language this relation is not a contour identity alone;
it uses the thermal density matrix and its analytic domain.  The fluctuation-
dissipation relation follows by applying the KMS relation to the spectral
decomposition of two-point functions.

\section{Path-Integral Status}

A Schwinger--Keldysh path integral is a coordinate representation of
\(Z[J_+,J_-]\).  Its fields are doubled,
\[
  \phi_+,\phi_-,
\]
with a matching condition at the final time that implements the trace.  For a
regulated bosonic theory, the phase is
\[
  \ii S[\phi_+]-\ii S[\phi_-]
  +\ii\int J_+O[\phi_+]-\ii\int J_-O[\phi_-]
\]
plus the coordinate representation of \(\rho_0\).  Gauge theories require
BRST or BV data on the doubled contour, including ghost, antifield, and
boundary conditions compatible with \(Z[J,J]=1\).

\begin{openproblem}[Real-time continuum construction]
\label{op:real-time-sk-continuum-construction}
Construct Schwinger--Keldysh generating functionals for interacting continuum
QFTs in a topology strong enough to derive retarded correlators, KMS
relations, and transport limits.  The construction must state the initial
state, operator domains, regulator, contour matching, and the sense in which
unitarity and positivity survive the continuum limit.
\end{openproblem}
