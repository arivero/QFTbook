\chapter{Global Forms and Higher-Form Symmetry}
\label{ch:global-forms-higher-form-symmetry}

\ConventionsMixed

The local Lie algebra of currents is one layer of the symmetry data of a QFT.
The global form of an internal symmetry group, the allowed line and surface
operators, and the spectrum of charged extended objects are also part of the
quantum data.

\section{Global Form of a Gauge Group}

Let \(\mathfrak g\) be a compact semisimple Lie algebra with simply connected
group \(G_{\rm sc}\) and center \(Z(G_{\rm sc})\).  A connected compact group
with Lie algebra \(\mathfrak g\) is
\[
  G=G_{\rm sc}/\Gamma,
  \qquad
  \Gamma\subset Z(G_{\rm sc}).
\]
The choice of \(\Gamma\) changes which Wilson lines are genuine line
operators.  A representation of \(G_{\rm sc}\) descends to a representation
of \(G\) exactly when \(\Gamma\) acts trivially.  Thus the global form is
measured by the lattice of line charges together with the local curvature
term in the Lagrangian.

\begin{definition}[Genuine line operator]
\label{def:genuine-line-operator}
A line operator \(L(C)\) supported on an embedded curve \(C\) is genuine if it
is defined without choosing an auxiliary surface ending on \(C\).  If a
surface \(S\) with \(\partial S=C\) is part of the definition, the operator is
a boundary of a higher-dimensional defect rather than an autonomous line
operator.
\end{definition}

\section{Higher-Form Symmetry}

\begin{definition}[\(p\)-form global symmetry]
\label{def:p-form-global-symmetry}
A \(p\)-form global symmetry in a \(D\)-dimensional QFT is represented by
topological operators \(U_g(M^{D-p-1})\), labelled by group elements \(g\) and
supported on closed codimension-\((p+1)\) manifolds, acting on charged
\(p\)-dimensional operators.  Topological means that correlation functions
are invariant under smooth deformations of \(M\) that avoid charged
insertions and do not cross singularities or boundaries.
\end{definition}

For an abelian \(p\)-form symmetry, a charged \(p\)-operator \(W_q(\Sigma^p)\)
satisfies
\[
  U_\alpha(M^{D-p-1})W_q(\Sigma^p)
  =
  \exp\!\left(\ii\alpha q\,{\rm Link}(M,\Sigma)\right)
  W_q(\Sigma^p)
\]
inside correlation functions, where the linking number is defined after
choosing orientations and a homology class.  This formula is the global
operator statement behind selection rules for extended objects.

\section{Center Symmetry in Yang--Mills}

For pure \(SU(N)\) Yang--Mills, the center \(Z_N\) acts as a one-form symmetry
on Wilson lines.  If \(W_R(C)=\operatorname{tr}_R{\rm P}\exp\int_C A\), then
the charge is the \(N\)-ality of \(R\).  Dynamical matter in a representation
of nonzero \(N\)-ality explicitly breaks this one-form symmetry because a
Wilson line can end on a dynamical charged field.  Thus confinement,
screening, and the global form of the gauge group are statements about the
category of allowed extended operators and endpoints.

\begin{openproblem}[Global structure from continuum local data]
\label{op:global-structure-from-local-data}
Determine, for a given continuum QFT specified by local fields and local
correlators, what additional data recover the full lattice of genuine line,
surface, and defect operators.  Local Wightman fields determine local
correlators; global form and extended-operator completeness require further
information about charged sectors, boundary conditions, and topological
defect fusion.
\end{openproblem}
