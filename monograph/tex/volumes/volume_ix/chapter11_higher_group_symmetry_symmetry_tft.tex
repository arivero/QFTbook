\chapter{Higher-Group Symmetry And Symmetry TQFT}
\label{ch:global-higher-group-symmetry-tft}

\ConventionsMixed

Higher-form symmetries and ordinary symmetries may combine into a single
background-field structure.  The combined object is not merely a product of
groups; its gauge transformations and anomalies may be linked by Postnikov
classes.  This chapter defines higher-group backgrounds and then explains
the symmetry-TQFT organization of boundary conditions and gauging operations.

\section{Two-Group Backgrounds}

Let \(G_0\) be an ordinary finite internal symmetry and let \(A\) be an
abelian one-form symmetry.  A two-group is specified by an action of \(G_0\)
on \(A\) and a Postnikov class
\[
  \beta\in H^3(BG_0,A).
\]
On a triangulated spacetime \(M\), a background consists of a \(G_0\)-valued
one-cocycle \(g\) and an \(A\)-valued two-cochain \(B\) obeying
\[
  \delta_g B=\beta(g).
\]
Here \(\delta_g\) is the coboundary twisted by the \(G_0\)-action on \(A\).
Thus \(B\) is not closed when the ordinary background \(g\) has nontrivial
Postnikov obstruction.

\section{Gauge Transformations}

A \(G_0\) gauge transformation changes \(g\) by a coboundary.  The two-form
background \(B\) must transform at the same time so that
\(\delta_gB=\beta(g)\) remains valid.  A one-form gauge transformation with
\(A\)-valued one-cochain \(\lambda\) acts by
\[
  B\mapsto B+\delta_g\lambda .
\]
If \(\beta\ne0\), the ordinary and one-form transformations do not commute as
independent operations on the background fields.  The physical statement is
that charged line operators and local operators transform in a single
higher-group representation problem.

\section{Anomaly Functionals}

An anomaly of a two-group symmetry in \(D\) dimensions is an invertible
\((D+1)\)-dimensional action for \((g,B)\).  A basic term is
\[
  S_{\mathrm{anom}}[g,B]
  =
  2\pi i\int_X
  \langle B\smile \gamma(g)\rangle ,
\]
where \(\gamma(g)\) is a \(G_0\)-dependent cochain with values in the Pontryagin
dual of \(A\).  Gauge invariance on closed \(X\) imposes a cocycle condition
using \(\delta_gB=\beta(g)\).  If \(X\) has boundary, the boundary QFT
partition function is a section of the anomaly line over background fields.

\section{Symmetry TQFT}

For a \(D\)-dimensional QFT with finite symmetry, one can package background
fields, gauging, and duality defects in a \((D+1)\)-dimensional topological
theory.  A physical QFT is then represented by a boundary condition
\[
  \mathfrak B_Q
\]
for the symmetry TQFT.  Coupling to a background corresponds to ending the
topological theory on a background boundary.  Gauging corresponds to replacing
that boundary condition by a summing boundary condition.

For an ordinary finite group \(G\), the symmetry TQFT is finite \(G\)-gauge
theory in one higher dimension.  For higher groups, the bulk field space is
the groupoid of pairs \((g,B)\) satisfying the Postnikov constraint.  The
fusion of topological defects in the QFT is inherited from the fusion of
topological boundary-changing operators in the bulk.

\section{Condensation And Gauging}

Let \(\mathcal C\) be the category or higher category of topological defects
describing a finite symmetry.  Gauging a sub-symmetry is condensation of a
commutative algebra object
\[
  A_{\mathrm{cond}}\in\mathcal C .
\]
The gauged theory has boundary condition obtained by allowing endpoints of
the condensed defects.  If the algebra object is obstructed by an anomaly,
the condensation exists only after coupling to an appropriate bulk or after
choosing a trivialization of the anomaly.

\section{Example: Mixed Zero- And One-Form Data}

Consider a four-dimensional gauge theory with gauge group \(SU(N)\) and a
charge conjugation symmetry acting on the center one-form symmetry
\[
  A=\mathbb Z_N
\]
by inversion.  A background for the ordinary symmetry changes the local
system in which the \(A\)-valued two-form background lives.  If a theta-angle
or discrete theta term changes under charge conjugation, the anomaly is a
five-dimensional functional of the combined background.  The line-operator
lattice then transforms as a module over the two-group rather than as a
separate representation of each symmetry.

\section{Comparison Ledger}

The symmetry-TQFT description is not a replacement for constructing the QFT.
It records:
\[
\begin{gathered}
  \text{allowed background fields},\qquad
  \text{anomaly lines},\\
  \text{gauging and condensation operations},\qquad
  \text{topological defect fusion}.
\end{gathered}
\]
Local correlators, Hilbert space, stress tensor, and dynamical phase
structure must still be supplied by the QFT itself.

\begin{openproblem}[Higher-group examples]
\label{op:higher-group-examples}
Construct interacting continuum QFT examples whose higher-group backgrounds,
symmetry TQFT, defect fusion, anomaly inflow, and local-operator action are
all derived from a single regulator or reconstruction framework.
\end{openproblem}
