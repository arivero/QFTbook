\chapter{Eta Invariants And Global Anomalies}
\label{ch:curved-eta-invariants-global-anomalies}

\ConventionsEuclidean

This chapter continues the determinant-line construction of
Chapter~\ref{ch:curved-background-gauge-fields-index-theory}.  The local
anomaly polynomial controls infinitesimal background variations; eta
invariants control phases under large diffeomorphisms, large gauge
transformations, and loops in background-field space.

\section{Eta Function}

Let \(Y\) be a closed odd-dimensional Riemannian spin manifold with background
gauge field \(A\), and let \(D_Y\) be a self-adjoint Dirac operator coupled to
the chosen bundle.  Its nonzero eigenvalues are real and discrete.  The eta
function is
\[
  \eta_{D_Y}(s)
  =
  \sum_{\lambda\neq0}
  \operatorname{sign}(\lambda)|\lambda|^{-s},
  \qquad \operatorname{Re}s\gg1 .
\]
For Dirac-type operators this function has a meromorphic continuation that is
regular at \(s=0\).  Define
\[
  h(D_Y)=\dim\ker D_Y,
  \qquad
  \xi(D_Y)=\frac{\eta_{D_Y}(0)+h(D_Y)}{2}.
\]
The reduced invariant \(\xi(D_Y)\) is the phase datum that enters fermion
determinants.

\section{Atiyah-Patodi-Singer Boundary Formula}

Let \(X\) be a compact even-dimensional spin manifold with boundary
\(\partial X=Y\), and assume the metric and background fields have product
form near \(Y\).  Let \(D_X^+\) be the chiral Dirac operator with
Atiyah-Patodi-Singer boundary condition.  Then
\[
  \operatorname{ind}D_X^+
  =
  \int_X
  \widehat A(R)\operatorname{ch}(F)\big|_{\dim X}
  -
  \xi(D_Y).
\]
The boundary condition is spectral: boundary spinors are projected onto the
span of eigenvectors of \(D_Y\) with negative eigenvalue, with the kernel
treated by the convention included in \(h(D_Y)\).

\begin{proposition}[Metric variation of the reduced eta invariant]
\label{prop:eta-variation-local}
Along a smooth path of background fields on \(Y\) for which \(\dim\ker D_Y\)
is constant, the variation of \(\xi(D_Y)\) is given modulo integers by the
integral of the local descent form obtained from
\(\widehat A(R)\operatorname{ch}(F)\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let the path parameter be \(t\in[0,1]\), and form the cylinder
\(X=[0,1]\times Y\) with the interpolating background.  Apply the
Atiyah-Patodi-Singer formula to the cylinder.  The index is an integer.  The
bulk integral is the transgression of the characteristic form along the path,
and the two boundary contributions are the reduced eta invariants at the
endpoints with opposite orientations.  Hence the change of \(\xi\) equals the
bulk transgression modulo an integer.  Differentiating with respect to the
path parameter gives the local descent variation.
\end{proof}

\section{Determinant-Line Holonomy}

Let \(\mathcal B\) be a smooth space of background metrics and gauge fields
modulo based gauge transformations.  A family of chiral Dirac operators over
\(\mathcal B\) defines a determinant line bundle
\[
  \operatorname{Det}D\longrightarrow \mathcal B .
\]
For a loop \(\gamma:S^1\to\mathcal B\), form the mapping torus \(Y_\gamma\).
The natural connection on the determinant line has holonomy
\[
  \operatorname{Hol}_\gamma(\operatorname{Det}D)
  =
  \exp\!\left(-2\pi\ii\,\xi(D_{Y_\gamma})\right)
\]
with the sign fixed by the orientation convention for the mapping torus and
the chiral determinant.  This formula is the global anomaly diagnostic: a
nontrivial holonomy means that the fermion partition function is a section of
a nontrivial line bundle over background-field space.

\section{Dai-Freed Phase}

For an odd-dimensional boundary theory, the exponentiated eta invariant of an
even-dimensional bulk supplies a canonical phase factor
\[
  \exp\!\left(2\pi\ii\,\xi(D_X)\right)
\]
whose variation cancels the determinant-line variation of the boundary
fermion when the bulk and boundary bundles are paired correctly.  The combined
bulk-boundary partition function is a number rather than a section, provided
the background fields extend over the bulk and the relevant bordism
ambiguity has trivial phase.  This is the eta-invariant form of anomaly
inflow for global anomalies.

\section{Relation To Local Anomaly Polynomials}

If \(Y\) is the mapping torus of an infinitesimal loop in background space,
Proposition~\ref{prop:eta-variation-local} reduces the eta phase to the
integral of the descent form associated with the local anomaly polynomial.
Large loops require the full reduced eta invariant.  Therefore local anomaly
cancellation is the vanishing of the curvature of the determinant line, while
global anomaly cancellation is the triviality of its holonomy on all loops in
background-field space.

\section{Interacting Theories}

For free fermions, determinant lines and eta invariants are built from Dirac
operators.  For an interacting QFT, the analogous object is the anomaly line
over background-field space, defined by the phase ambiguity of the regulated
partition function and by its response to background gluing.  A fully
developed theory must construct this line without relying on a quadratic
fermion determinant and must compare it with invertible bulk field theories.

\begin{openproblem}[Global anomaly line for interacting QFT]
\label{op:global-anomaly-line-interacting-qft}
Construct the anomaly line of an interacting QFT over the space of background
fields, prove its local curvature and global holonomy formulae, and identify
the conditions under which it is represented by an invertible bulk theory.
\end{openproblem}
