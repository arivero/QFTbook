\chapter{Semiclassical Backreaction And Stress-Tensor Fluctuations}
\label{ch:curved-semiclassical-backreaction}

\ConventionsLorentzian

QFT on a fixed curved background supplies a renormalized stress tensor.  Once
that stress tensor is used as a source for geometry, the problem changes:
the metric is no longer merely a background parameter.  This chapter records
the semiclassical equation, its renormalization ambiguities, and the
stress-tensor fluctuation data needed to state a controlled backreaction
problem.

\section{Renormalized Stress Tensor}

Let \((M,g)\) be globally hyperbolic and let \(\omega\) be a Hadamard state
of a QFT with locally covariant stress tensor
\[
  T_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{ren}}[g].
\]
The expectation value
\[
  \langle T_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{ren}}(x)\rangle_{\omega,g}
\]
is a distributional tensor that is smooth for Hadamard states after the
point-splitting subtraction and local curvature counterterms have been fixed.
Conservation is imposed as
\[
  \nabla^\mu \langle T_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{ren}}\rangle_{\omega,g}=0
\]
when background equations and anomalies permit it.

\section{Semiclassical Einstein Equation}

The semiclassical equation is
\[
  \frac{1}{8\pi G_N}
  \left(
    G_{\mu\nu}+\Lambda g_{\mu\nu}
  \right)
  +
  \alpha\,H^{(1)}_{\mu\nu}
  +
  \beta\,H^{(2)}_{\mu\nu}
  =
  \langle T_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{ren}}\rangle_{\omega,g}.
\]
Here \(H^{(1)}_{\mu\nu}\) and \(H^{(2)}_{\mu\nu}\) are metric variations of
local curvature actions such as
\[
  \int R^2\,d\mathrm{vol}_g,\qquad
  \int R_{\rho\sigma}R^{\rho\sigma}\,d\mathrm{vol}_g .
\]
The constants \(G_N,\Lambda,\alpha,\beta\) are coordinates of the
gravitational effective action.  Changing the stress-tensor renormalization
scheme changes these coordinates.

\section{Linear Response}

Let \(g_{\mu\nu}\mapsto g_{\mu\nu}+h_{\mu\nu}\).  The variation of the
stress-tensor expectation has the form
\[
  \delta\langle T_{\mu\nu}(x)\rangle
  =
  \frac12\int_M d\mathrm{vol}_g(y)\,
  G^{\mathrm{ret}}_{\mu\nu,\rho\sigma}(x,y)h^{\rho\sigma}(y)
  +
  \delta T_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{loc}}(x).
\]
The retarded kernel is determined by the commutator distribution
\[
  G^{\mathrm{ret}}_{\mu\nu,\rho\sigma}(x,y)
  =
  -i\Theta(x\succ y)
  \omega\bigl([T_{\mu\nu}(x),T_{\rho\sigma}(y)]\bigr),
\]
with contact terms fixed by the same local renormalization coordinates that
define \(T_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{ren}}\).  The symbol \(x\succ y\) means that
\(x\) lies in the causal future of \(y\).

\section{Noise Kernel}

Stress-tensor fluctuations are encoded by the symmetrized connected
two-point distribution
\[
  N_{\mu\nu,\rho\sigma}(x,y)
  =
  \frac12\,
  \omega\bigl(\{T_{\mu\nu}(x),T_{\rho\sigma}(y)\}\bigr)_{\mathrm{conn}} .
\]
This distribution is singular at \(x=y\).  It is defined by point splitting
or by local Wick-product renormalization, with contact terms again fixed by
local covariance and Ward identities.  Conservation away from the diagonal is
\[
  \nabla_x^\mu N_{\mu\nu,\rho\sigma}(x,y)=0,
  \qquad
  \nabla_y^\rho N_{\mu\nu,\rho\sigma}(x,y)=0 .
\]

\section{Einstein--Langevin Equation}

A stochastic semiclassical approximation introduces a classical random tensor
\(\xi_{\mu\nu}\) with
\[
  \mathbb E[\xi_{\mu\nu}(x)]=0,\qquad
  \mathbb E[\xi_{\mu\nu}(x)\xi_{\rho\sigma}(y)]
  =
  N_{\mu\nu,\rho\sigma}(x,y).
\]
The metric perturbation then satisfies
\[
  \delta E_{\mu\nu}[h]
  -
  \frac12\int G^{\mathrm{ret}}_{\mu\nu,\rho\sigma}h^{\rho\sigma}
  =
  \xi_{\mu\nu},
\]
where \(\delta E_{\mu\nu}\) is the variation of the local gravitational side
of the semiclassical equation.  This equation is meaningful only after the
state, stress-tensor renormalization, contact terms, and stochastic
distribution have been specified.

\section{Validity Conditions}

The semiclassical approximation requires more than small curvature.  A
controlled statement includes:
\[
\begin{gathered}
  \ell_{\mathrm{curv}}\gg \ell_{\mathrm{UV}},\\
  \text{renormalized stress tensor small compared with gravitational cutoff
  scales},\\
  \text{stress-tensor fluctuations small in the observables being tested},\\
  \text{state stability under the metric perturbations considered},\\
  \text{hyperbolic well-posedness after higher-curvature terms are treated}.
\end{gathered}
\]
Higher-derivative terms may introduce additional solutions if treated as
exact equations beyond their effective-field-theory domain.  A reduction of
order or an equivalent EFT prescription is part of the semiclassical datum.

\section{Backreaction Ledger}

A backreaction problem is specified by
\[
  (M,g,\omega,T_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{ren}},
  G_N,\Lambda,\alpha,\beta,\mathcal B),
\]
where \(\mathcal B\) denotes boundary or initial data.  The output is not a
single number but a solution space with stability, state-dependence, and
renormalization-coordinate dependence.  Black-hole evaporation, cosmological
particle creation, and Casimir backreaction are applications of this datum.

\begin{openproblem}[Backreaction theorem]
\label{op:backreaction-theorem}
Formulate and prove a semiclassical backreaction theorem for an interacting
Hadamard QFT with renormalized stress tensor, controlled stress-tensor
fluctuations, local covariance, and a hyperbolic gravitational EFT evolution.
\end{openproblem}
