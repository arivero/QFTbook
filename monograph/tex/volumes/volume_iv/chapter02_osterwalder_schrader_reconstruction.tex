\section{Euclidean Correlation Data}

Let \(E=\mathbb R^D\) be Euclidean space with coordinates
\[
  x=(\tau,\mathbf x),\qquad \mathbf x\in\mathbb R^{D-1}.
\]
Euclidean correlation functions are distributions
\[
  S_{a_1\cdots a_n}(x_1,\ldots,x_n)
\]
on \(E^n\), with field labels \(a_j\).  In a construction by a Euclidean
measure, they are moments of random distributions.  In an axiomatic Euclidean
formulation, the hierarchy \(\{S_n\}\) is primary data.

Let \(\theta\) be time reflection:
\[
  \theta(\tau,\mathbf x)=(-\tau,\mathbf x).
\]
For a test function \(f_n\) supported in the ordered positive-time region
\[
  0<\tau_1<\cdots<\tau_n ,
\]
define the reflected adjoint test function \(\Theta f_n\) by reversing the
order, reflecting all arguments, complex conjugating, and applying the field
adjunction.  Reflection positivity is the requirement
\begin{equation}
  \sum_{m,n} S_{m+n}\bigl(\Theta f_m,f_n\bigr)\ge0
  \label{eq:os-reflection-positivity}
\end{equation}
for every finite collection of such positive-time test functions.

The other Osterwalder--Schrader data are Euclidean covariance, permutation
symmetry, regularity and growth assumptions sufficient for analytic
continuation, and a normalization \(S_0=1\).  Clustering is added when the
target theory is required to have a unique vacuum.

\section{Hilbert Space From Positive Time}

Let \(\mathcal E_+\) be the vector space of finite sequences
\[
  F=(f_0,f_1,f_2,\ldots)
\]
where only finitely many \(f_n\) are nonzero and each \(f_n\) is supported in
positive Euclidean time.  Define a sesquilinear form by
\[
  \langle F,G\rangle_{\rm OS}
  =
  \sum_{m,n} S_{m+n}(\Theta f_m,g_n).
\]
Reflection positivity says that this form is nonnegative.  Let
\[
  \mathcal N=\{F\in\mathcal E_+\mid \langle F,F\rangle_{\rm OS}=0\}.
\]
The reconstructed Hilbert space is
\[
  \Hilb_{\rm OS}
  =
  \overline{\mathcal E_+/\mathcal N}.
\]
The vector represented by \(F=(1,0,0,\ldots)\) is the vacuum.

Euclidean time translations by \(t\ge0\) preserve positive-time support and
therefore define a contraction semigroup on \(\Hilb_{\rm OS}\):
\[
  T(t)=\ee^{-tH},\qquad H\ge0.
\]
The self-adjoint generator \(H\) is the Lorentzian Hamiltonian.  Spatial
translations and rotations act unitarily, and the analytic continuation of
Euclidean rotations supplies the Lorentz boosts under the OS hypotheses.

\section{Reconstruction Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Osterwalder--Schrader reconstruction]
Let \(\{S_n\}_{n\ge0}\) be Euclidean distributions satisfying Euclidean
covariance, permutation symmetry, reflection positivity, Hermiticity,
normalization, and the regularity and growth assumptions required for
tempered reconstruction.  Then there exists a Wightman theory whose
Schwinger functions are \(\{S_n\}\) at noncoincident Euclidean points.  The
reconstructed Wightman functions are obtained as boundary values of analytic
continuations of the \(S_n\).
\end{theorem}

The theorem has two distinct pieces.  Reflection positivity constructs the
Hilbert space and positive Hamiltonian.  Growth and analyticity assumptions
control the distributional boundary values and produce Wightman functions in
the intended test-function class.  In the tempered case, this target class is
\(\mathcal S'(M^n)\).  In larger analytic frameworks, such as hyperfunction
versions of reconstruction, the target distribution class is correspondingly
larger and the growth hypotheses are changed.

\section{Euclidean Measures and Generating Functionals}

Suppose a Euclidean theory is given by a probability measure \(\mu\) on a
space of distributions \(\varphi\), with generating functional
\[
  Z_E[J]=\int \exp\left(\int_E J(x)\varphi(x)\,\dd^Dx\right)\,\dd\mu(\varphi)
\]
for real test functions \(J\) in a neighborhood of the origin.  The Schwinger
functions are moments:
\[
  S_n(x_1,\ldots,x_n)
  =
  \int \varphi(x_1)\cdots\varphi(x_n)\,\dd\mu(\varphi).
\]
Reflection positivity is then a property of the measure together with the
chosen time reflection.  It is stable under lattice approximations only when
the regulator action, observable insertions, and continuum limit preserve the
positive-time quadratic form.

\section{Relation To Wick Rotation}

For time-ordered Green functions in perturbation theory, Wick rotation was
used earlier as a contour and boundary-value operation.  OS reconstruction is
the nonperturbative counterpart at the level of complete Euclidean
correlation data.  It supplies the Hilbert space, Hamiltonian, vacuum, and
Lorentzian Wightman functions from Euclidean data satisfying precise
positivity and growth conditions.

The framework therefore explains exactly which Euclidean information is needed
to recover a Lorentzian theory.  The answer is the full compatible hierarchy
of Schwinger distributions together with the OS structural properties, not
only a formal Euclidean action.
