\chapter{Topological Sigma Models}
\label{ch:tqft-topological-sigma-models}

\ConventionsEuclidean

Topological sigma models are cohomological QFTs whose bosonic field is a map
from a Riemann surface to a target manifold.  The A-model and B-model use
different geometric data on the target and therefore produce different
cohomological observables.

\section{Sigma-Model Field Space}

Let \(\Sigma\) be a closed oriented Riemann surface and \(X\) a compact
K\"ahler manifold.  The bosonic configuration space is
\[
  \operatorname{Map}(\Sigma,X).
\]
At a map \(\phi:\Sigma\to X\), tangent vectors are sections of
\[
  \Gamma(\Sigma,\phi^\ast TX).
\]
Fermionic variables in the cohomological model are parity-shifted sections of
bundles built from \(\phi^\ast TX\), \(\phi^\ast T^{1,0}X\), and
\(\phi^\ast T^{0,1}X\).  The field space is therefore a locally super-ringed
space over \(\operatorname{Map}(\Sigma,X)\), with fermionic directions encoded
by its structure sheaf.

\section{A-Model BRST Complex}

For the A-model, the observables are built from de Rham classes on \(X\).
Let \(\psi^i\) denote the fermionic variable corresponding to \(d\phi^i\) in
local coordinates on \(X\).  The scalar cohomological differential acts by
\[
  Q\phi^i=\psi^i,\qquad Q\psi^i=0
\]
on zero-form observables.  If \(\alpha\in\Omega^p(X)\), define
\[
  \mathcal O_\alpha(x)
  =
  \frac{1}{p!}\alpha_{i_1\cdots i_p}(\phi(x))
  \psi^{i_1}(x)\cdots\psi^{i_p}(x).
\]
Then
\[
  Q\mathcal O_\alpha=\mathcal O_{d\alpha}.
\]
Thus local A-model observables are represented by de Rham cohomology classes
of \(X\), modulo the usual proviso that contact terms and descendants require
a source convention.

\section{Localization to Holomorphic Maps}

Let \(J_\Sigma\) and \(J_X\) be the complex structures on \(\Sigma\) and
\(X\).  The instanton equation is
\[
  \bar\partial_J\phi
  =
  \frac12(d\phi+J_X\circ d\phi\circ J_\Sigma)=0 .
\]
The bosonic action contains
\[
  \frac{1}{2e^2}\int_\Sigma |d\phi|^2\,d\operatorname{vol}
  +\ii\int_\Sigma \phi^\ast(B+\ii\omega),
\]
where \(\omega\) is the K\"ahler form and \(B\) is a closed two-form background.
After the topological twist, the positive term is \(Q\)-exact up to the
topological area
\[
  \int_\Sigma\phi^\ast\omega .
\]
The localization locus is therefore the moduli space
\[
  \mathcal M_{\beta}(\Sigma,X)
  =
  \{\phi:\Sigma\to X\,:\,\bar\partial_J\phi=0,\ [\phi(\Sigma)]=\beta\}.
\]
For compact smooth moduli spaces without excess zero modes, a genus-\(g\)
correlator of A-model observables is the finite-dimensional integral
\[
  \sum_{\beta}
  q^\beta
  \int_{\mathcal M_{\beta}(\Sigma,X)}
  \bigwedge_a \operatorname{ev}_a^\ast\alpha_a ,
\]
where \(\operatorname{ev}_a\) evaluates a holomorphic map at the marked point
supporting \(\alpha_a\).  Singular moduli spaces require a virtual
fundamental class or an equivalent finite-dimensional replacement of the
localized integral.

\section{B-Model Observables}

For the B-model, \(X\) must be Calabi-Yau for the usual anomaly cancellation
on closed worldsheets.  Local observables are represented by
\[
  H^q(X,\wedge^p T^{1,0}X).
\]
In local coordinates, if \(\eta^{\bar i}\) is the fermionic coordinate for
\(\Omega^{0,1}\) and \(\theta_i\) is the fermionic coordinate dual to
\(\partial/\partial z^i\), a polyvector-valued \((0,q)\)-form
\[
  \mu
  =
  \mu^{i_1\cdots i_p}{}_{\bar j_1\cdots\bar j_q}
  d\bar z^{\bar j_1}\cdots d\bar z^{\bar j_q}
  \otimes
  \partial_{i_1}\wedge\cdots\wedge\partial_{i_p}
\]
gives an observable
\[
  \mathcal O_\mu
  =
  \mu^{i_1\cdots i_p}{}_{\bar j_1\cdots\bar j_q}(\phi)
  \eta^{\bar j_1}\cdots\eta^{\bar j_q}
  \theta_{i_1}\cdots\theta_{i_p}.
\]
The cohomological differential acts as \(\bar\partial\) on this complex, so
the local B-model state space is the corresponding Dolbeault cohomology.

\section{Dependence on Target Geometry}

The A-model correlation functions depend on the symplectic/K\"ahler class and
on the \(B\)-field through \(q^\beta\).  Complex-structure deformations change
the representative action by \(Q\)-exact terms when the anomaly conditions are
satisfied.  The B-model reverses this pattern: its local observables and
correlators depend on complex-structure data, while K\"ahler deformations are
\(Q\)-exact.  This statement is a cohomological Ward identity; its use in a
continuum QFT requires a regulator preserving the corresponding \(Q\)-symmetry
and a compactification of zero-mode integrals.

\begin{openproblem}[Topological sigma model as continuum QFT]
\label{op:topological-sigma-model-continuum-qft}
Formulate A- and B-models as continuum cohomological QFTs with explicit
integration cycles, compactified zero-mode spaces, anomaly line, and
gluing-compatible correlation functionals, rather than only as
finite-dimensional enumerative prescriptions.
\end{openproblem}
