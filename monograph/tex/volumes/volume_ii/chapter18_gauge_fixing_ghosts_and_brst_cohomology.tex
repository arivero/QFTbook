\chapter{Gauge Fixing, Ghosts, and BRST Cohomology}
\label{ch:volume-ii-18}

\section{Faddeev--Popov Determinants}

Nonabelian gauge fixing introduces a field-dependent Jacobian. For a gauge
condition \(F(A)=0\),
\[
  1=\int Dg\,\delta(F(A^g))\det\left(\frac{\delta F(A^g)}{\delta g}\right).
\]
The determinant is represented by anticommuting ghost fields \(c,\bar c\).
For covariant gauge,
\[
  \mathcal L_{\rm gh}
  =
  \bar c^a(-\partial^\mu D_\mu^{ab})c^b .
\]
Ghosts are not physical particles. They are local fields that represent the
gauge-fixing Jacobian and enforce unitarity of the physical sector.

\section{BRST Symmetry}

Gauge-fixed Yang--Mills theory has a fermionic symmetry \(s\):
\[
  sA_\mu=D_\mu c,\qquad
  sc=-\frac12[c,c],\qquad
  s\bar c=B,\qquad
  sB=0.
\]
It is nilpotent:
\[
  s^2=0.
\]
The gauge-fixed action may be written as the gauge-invariant action plus a
BRST-exact term:
\[
  S_{\rm gf+gh}=S_{\rm YM}+s\Psi.
\]

\section{Physical States}

BRST quantization identifies physical states with cohomology:
\[
  \Hilb_{\rm phys}
  =
  \frac{\ker Q_{\rm BRST}}{\operatorname{im}Q_{\rm BRST}}.
\]
States differing by a BRST-exact vector are physically equivalent. This is the
gauge-theory analogue of quotienting by gauge redundancy, but now implemented
inside a local gauge-fixed quantum formalism.

\section{Ward Identities}

BRST symmetry implies Slavnov--Taylor identities. They replace naive gauge
invariance in the gauge-fixed theory and constrain counterterms. In
renormalization, the demand is not that every intermediate expression be gauge
invariant, but that the renormalized theory preserve BRST cohomology and its
identities.
