\chapter{Global Anomalies, Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking, and Pions}
\label{ch:volume-ii-21}

\section{Global Symmetries Coupled to Background Fields}

A continuous global symmetry \(G_{\mathrm f}\) acts on local operators and on
the Hilbert space, but its gauge field is not integrated over in the original
theory.  To test the symmetry, introduce a nondynamical background connection
\(B\) for \(G_{\mathrm f}\) and define the generating functional
\[
  Z[B]
  =
  \int [D\Phi]\,
  \exp\{\ii S[\Phi;B]\},
\]
where \(\Phi\) denotes all dynamical fields.  A global anomaly is present when
the background-field functional cannot be made invariant under background
gauge transformations by adding local counterterms depending only on \(B\).

This is compatible with the original theory because \(B\) is not a dynamical
gauge field.  The anomaly is instead an invariant datum of the global symmetry
action.  In modern language it may be represented by an invertible field
theory in one dimension higher whose boundary variation cancels the variation
of \(Z[B]\).  The present chapter uses only the consequence needed for QCD:
the anomaly of an exact global symmetry is preserved under RG flow.

\section{Anomaly Matching}

Let a microscopic theory with global symmetry \(G_{\mathrm f}\) flow to an
infrared theory.  Couple both descriptions to the same background \(B\).  If
\[
  Z_{\mathrm{UV}}[B]\longmapsto
  \ee^{\ii\mathcal A[B,g]}Z_{\mathrm{UV}}[B]
\]
under a background gauge transformation \(g\), then the infrared description
must transform with the same phase after possible local counterterms are
matched:
\[
  Z_{\mathrm{IR}}[B]\longmapsto
  \ee^{\ii\mathcal A[B,g]}Z_{\mathrm{IR}}[B].
\]
This is 't Hooft anomaly matching.  It is a statement about exact symmetries
and their background response.  The matching may be realized by massless
fermions, by Goldstone fields with a Wess--Zumino--Witten functional, by a
topological field theory, or by a combination of these ingredients.

\section{Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking}

Consider a quantum theory in infinite spatial volume with an exact continuous
global symmetry group \(G\).  Let \(\mathcal H\) be the Hilbert space obtained
from one chosen phase, and let \(Q_A\) be the conserved charges associated
with generators \(T_A\in\operatorname{Lie}(G)\) whenever the charges are
well-defined on local states.  A vacuum \(\ket{\Omega}\) preserves the
subgroup
\[
  H=\{h\in G:U(h)\ket{\Omega}=\ket{\Omega}\}.
\]
The symmetry is spontaneously broken from \(G\) to \(H\) when \(H\) is a
proper subgroup of \(G\).  The broken generators span a complement
\[
  \mathfrak g=\mathfrak h\oplus\mathfrak m,
\]
where \(\mathfrak h=\operatorname{Lie}(H)\).

Goldstone fields are local coordinates on the coset manifold \(G/H\).  Choose
broken generators \(X_a\in\mathfrak m\) and write
\begin{equation}
  U_\pi(x)
  =
  \exp\left\{\frac{\ii}{f}\pi^a(x)X_a\right\}.
  \label{eq:goldstone-coset-coordinate}
\end{equation}
The fields \(\pi^a\) are not arbitrary scalar coordinates unrelated to the
symmetry; they parametrize displacements along the vacuum manifold.  The
leading local action contains derivatives because constant motion along
\(G/H\) changes the chosen vacuum but not the local energy density:
\begin{equation}
  S_{\mathrm{Goldstone}}
  =
  \frac{f^2}{2}
  \int g_{ab}(\pi)\,
  \partial_\mu\pi^a\partial^\mu\pi^b\,\dd^4x
  +\cdots ,
  \label{eq:goldstone-leading-action}
\end{equation}
where \(g_{ab}\) is a \(G\)-invariant metric on the coset and the ellipsis
denotes higher-derivative terms and explicit symmetry-breaking terms.

\section{Chiral Symmetry of Massless QCD}

For \(N_f\) massless quark flavors, the classical flavor symmetry of the QCD
Lagrangian is
\[
  U(N_f)_L\times U(N_f)_R .
\]
The axial \(U(1)_A\) factor is broken by the anomaly.  The nonanomalous
continuous symmetry is
\[
  SU(N_f)_L\times SU(N_f)_R\times U(1)_B ,
\]
where \(U(1)_B\) is baryon number.  The standard chiral symmetry-breaking
pattern is
\begin{equation}
  SU(N_f)_L\times SU(N_f)_R
  \longrightarrow
  SU(N_f)_V .
  \label{eq:qcd-chiral-breaking-pattern}
\end{equation}
The Goldstone field may be assembled into an \(SU(N_f)\)-valued matrix
\begin{equation}
  U(x)=\exp\left\{\frac{2\ii}{f_\pi}\pi^a(x)T^a\right\},
  \qquad
  U(x)\in SU(N_f),
  \label{eq:pion-matrix-field}
\end{equation}
transforming as
\begin{equation}
  U(x)\longmapsto L\,U(x)\,R^{-1},
  \qquad
  (L,R)\in SU(N_f)_L\times SU(N_f)_R .
  \label{eq:pion-chiral-transformation}
\end{equation}
The leading two-derivative chiral Lagrangian is
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal L_2
  =
  \frac{f_\pi^2}{4}
  \operatorname{Tr}
  \bigl(\partial_\mu U^\dagger\partial^\mu U\bigr).
  \label{eq:leading-chiral-lagrangian}
\end{equation}
Here \(\operatorname{Tr}\) is the flavor trace, and the normalization of
\(f_\pi\) is fixed by the axial-current matrix element convention used for
the pion fields.

\section{Mass Spurions and Pseudo-Goldstone Bosons}

Quark masses explicitly break the chiral symmetry.  Introduce a complex mass
matrix \(M\) and assign it the spurion transformation
\[
  M\longmapsto LMR^{-1}.
\]
Then the leading mass-dependent invariant in the pion effective Lagrangian is
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal L_{\mathrm{mass}}
  =
  \frac{f_\pi^2}{4}
  \operatorname{Tr}
  \bigl(\chi U^\dagger+U\chi^\dagger\bigr),
  \qquad
  \chi=2B_0M .
  \label{eq:chiral-mass-spurion}
\end{equation}
The constant \(B_0\) is a low-energy parameter.  When \(M\) is set to its
physical value, \eqref{eq:chiral-mass-spurion} gives pion masses and
interactions ordered by the same derivative and mass expansion.  For degenerate
light quark mass \(m_q\), the leading pion mass relation has the form
\[
  m_\pi^2=2B_0m_q
\]
in the normalization of \eqref{eq:chiral-mass-spurion}.

\section{The Wess--Zumino--Witten Functional}

The pion action must also reproduce the global anomalies of the microscopic
quarks.  For \(N_f\ge3\), extend \(U:M_4\to SU(N_f)\) to a map
\(\widetilde U:B_5\to SU(N_f)\), where \(\partial B_5=M_4\).  The
ungauged Wess--Zumino functional is
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma_{\mathrm{WZ}}[U]
  =
  \frac{1}{240\pi^2}
  \int_{B_5}
  \operatorname{Tr}
  \bigl(\widetilde U^{-1}\dd \widetilde U\bigr)^5 .
  \label{eq:wess-zumino-functional}
\end{equation}
The coefficient multiplying this functional in QCD is quantized and equals
\(N_c\) with the standard fundamental flavor normalization.  The quantization
ensures that the exponentiated action is independent of the chosen extension
\(\widetilde U\).

When the flavor symmetry is coupled to background gauge fields, the Wess--
Zumino functional is completed to the Wess--Zumino--Witten functional.  Its
background variation reproduces the chiral anomaly coefficient of the quark
theory.  In particular, after electromagnetism is embedded through the quark
charge matrix \(Q\), the functional contains the anomalous neutral-pion
coupling to two photons.  This is the effective-field-theory realization of
the anomaly matching statement: the low-energy pion fields carry the same
background response as the microscopic quarks.

\section{Conceptual Output of the Gauge-Dynamics Sequence}

The chapters from Yang--Mills theory through pions have constructed a single
logical chain.  Gauge fields are local connections; gauge fixing supplies
local coordinates and ghosts; BRST cohomology defines physical states and
operators in the gauge-fixed formalism; QCD renormalization gives
asymptotically free short-distance variables; anomalies determine exact
current identities and consistency conditions; global anomalies are preserved
along RG flow; and spontaneous symmetry breaking turns the anomaly constraints
into concrete terms in the Goldstone effective action.
